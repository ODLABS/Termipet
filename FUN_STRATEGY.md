# Termipets Fun Strategy

## Goal

Make the game feel more alive, more replayable, and more emotionally sticky without turning it into grindy menu spam.

---

## 1. Make the pet react to how you play

Right now mini-games mostly pay out stats / XP / coins. The next layer is to make the pet feel like a participant, not a skin over a progress bar.

### Ideas

- **Contextual reactions after each game**
  - Big win → pet celebrates
  - Loss → pet sulks or encourages you
  - Long streak → pet develops confidence
- **Pet-type preferences**
  - Cat likes memory / precision games
  - Dog likes action / chase games
  - Dragon likes risk / high-score games
  - Blob likes chill / survival games
- **Affinity bonus**
  - Playing a pet’s preferred games gives slightly more happiness or XP

### Why it helps

This makes play sessions feel personal instead of interchangeable.

---

## 2. Add progression to mini-games

The current game list is broad, but the experience can feel flat if every round is isolated.

### Ideas

- **Game mastery levels**
  - Bronze / Silver / Gold / Diamond per mini-game
- **Best-score tracking**
  - Show personal best beside each game
- **Unlock milestones**
  - Hit score thresholds to unlock cosmetics, toys, titles, or pet emotes
- **Difficulty bands**
  - Casual / Standard / Hard mode
  - Higher entry fee + higher payout on harder modes

### Why it helps

Players need short-term goals and a reason to return to the same game instead of sampling once and bouncing.

---

## 3. Turn the economy into interesting decisions

Pay-to-play is a good first step, but it becomes much more compelling when players choose between safety and risk.

### Ideas

- **Three stakes per game**
  - Low stake: cheap, safe, modest payout
  - Medium stake: current balance
  - High stake: expensive, best payout
- **Win streak multiplier**
  - Consecutive wins increase payout until a loss resets it
- **Daily featured game**
  - One game each day pays bonus XP / coins
- **Emergency fallback**
  - Keep one ultra-cheap game or a small daily allowance so broke players are not trapped

### Why it helps

This creates tension and strategy without requiring real-money mechanics.

---

## 4. Add session-level variety

The game loop is better when the player gets small rotating reasons to check back in.

### Ideas

- **Daily quests**
  - Win 2 games
  - Feed on time 3 times
  - Reach a combo threshold
- **Mood-based bonuses**
  - Happy pet earns more in play
  - Dirty / tired pet performs worse
- **Random events**
  - “Your pet found a lucky token”
  - “Zoomies active: action game rewards boosted for 10 minutes”

### Why it helps

This creates freshness without needing a giant content pipeline.

---

## 5. Increase emotional attachment

Terminal pets work when people feel guilty / proud / protective.

### Ideas

- **Memory moments**
  - “Mochi remembers your first win in Snake.”
- **Pet personality drift**
  - Playstyle slowly shapes temperament
- **Tiny narrative beats**
  - Level-up messages
  - Evolution diary snippets
  - Post-loss comfort messages

### Why it helps

The stronger the bond, the better the retention.

---

## 6. Best next implementation order

### Highest ROI first

1. **Track best scores per game**
2. **Add preferred games per pet type**
3. **Add daily featured game bonus**
4. **Add low / medium / high stake option**
5. **Add mastery tiers + unlock rewards**

---

## 7. Anti-grind guardrails

Do not let “more systems” make the game worse.

### Keep these constraints

- Sessions should stay **quick**
- Economy should feel **rewarding**, not punishing
- There should always be **some path back** from low coins
- Feedback should be **cute and readable**, not verbose

---

## Summary

The next version should optimize for:

- **pet personality**
- **score progression**
- **risk / reward choice**
- **daily replay hooks**
- **emotional attachment**

If implemented well, Termipets stops being “a bunch of mini-games in a CLI” and becomes “a pet you keep checking on because it feels alive.”
